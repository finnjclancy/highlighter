Privacy Policy

Last updated: 16 May 2026

This page describes how the Highlighter browser extension (the “Extension”) and the companion website at finnjclancy.github.io/highlighter (the “Gallery”) handle your information. The short version: nothing about you or your browsing leaves your device unless you explicitly choose to share a link.

What the extension stores

The Extension stores the following data locally in your browser using the standard chrome.storage APIs:

  • The text of any passages you choose to highlight, the page URL and title where you highlighted them, the colours, comments, and tags you assign, and the position of the highlight on the page.
  • Free-hand drawings you make with the Draw tool, scoped to the URL on which you drew them.
  • Your customised colour palette (synced via chrome.storage.sync across your signed-in Chrome profile if that feature is enabled in your browser).

This data never leaves your browser unless you take a deliberate action — either copying it (“Copy text”) or generating a share link (“Share live link”).

What the extension does not do
  • No analytics, telemetry, or usage tracking of any kind.
  • No advertising identifiers or fingerprinting.
  • No remote servers receive the contents of pages you visit.
  • No third-party scripts are loaded into the Extension.
  • No accounts, no sign-up, no log-in.
When you share a link

When you click Share live link, the Extension builds a URL that contains your highlights for the current page, gzip-compressed and base64-encoded into the URL itself. The URL is placed on your clipboard for you to send to whomever you choose. You control where it goes.

If a recipient opens the share link, their browser fetches the static Gallery page at finnjclancy.github.io/highlighter/v.html. As with any web page request, GitHub (which hosts the Gallery) records standard server access logs — typically IP address, user-agent, and timestamp — under its own privacy policy: GitHub Privacy Statement. The Gallery itself does not store, analyse, or transmit the highlights it renders; everything is decoded client-side in the recipient's browser from the URL.

Permissions, explained
  • storage — to save your highlights, drawings, and palette in your own browser.
  • activeTab, scripting — to inject the highlighting/drawing scripts into pages you are actively viewing.
  • clipboardWrite — to copy share links and exported text/Markdown to your clipboard.
  • host permissions: <all_urls> — so the Extension can apply highlights and drawings on any web page you choose to use it on. The Extension does not read or transmit page contents in the background.
Data retention and deletion

All highlight, drawing, and palette data lives in your browser's local storage. To delete it:

  • Open the Extension's library page and delete individual highlights or use Select All → Delete.
  • Or remove the Extension via chrome://extensions, which clears all of its stored data.
